AFROTC Detachment 165

We build next-generation wingmen, leaders, and warriors.

Welcome to Air Force ROTC Detachment 165! We are based out of Georgia Institute of Technology and host 11 cross-town schools in the Metro-Atlanta area. Detachment 165 has a long and rich history since its creation in 1920; in that time, we have commissioned over 1,700 officers into the United States Air Force. Our mission is to train and mentor future officers who will be next level leaders in the Air Force. Thank you for visiting our page, and I look forward to continuing our tradition of excellence with you.

— C/Col Bradley Jenkins, 165th Cadet Wing Commander, Spring 2019

What is AFROTC?

Air Force ROTC is a college program that prepares you to become a future officer in the United States Air Force. In AFROTC, you'll learn the fundamentals of effective leadership and apply those lessons in real-world environments and exercises. AFROTC cadets earn commissions as active-duty Air Force officers upon graduation and go on to serve in rewarding careers across the globe.
